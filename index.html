Who am I?

I am Aditya Rao, an aspiring engineer with a passion for solving complex challenges at the intersection of hardware and software. My academic journey in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at UC Berkeley, combined with hands-on work at SpaceX, Blue Origin, and CMU, has shaped my approach to tackling real-world problems.

My expertise lies in transforming innovative ideas into impactful, practical solutions, whether developing real-time telemetry systems, automating hardware testing, or advancing wearable medical devices. This portfolio reflects my dedication to engineering excellence and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Custom Firmware and iOS Integration for Medical Implants

The project involved designing and implementing Autotest drivers to streamline hardware testing processes, ensuring precision, reliability, and scalability for Falcon rocket components under demanding conditions. A critical aspect was automating sensor calibration to maintain the integrity of real-time data across extended testing cycles.

Key Contributions:

  • Methodology Development: Designed and implemented a novel loopback testing method to synchronize time sources effectively, overcoming both hardware and software limitations.
  • Validation: Conducted comprehensive oscilloscope testing and software simulations to ensure precision and consistency across various scenarios.
  • Team Collaboration: Worked closely with engineers and test operators to integrate the solution into broader lunar lander systems.

Impact:

  • Achieved precise latency measurements with 1.66-millisecond accuracy, surpassing initial targets.
  • Enhanced data synchronization reliability across lunar lander systems, contributing to mission success.
  • Demonstrated the scalability of the loopback testing method for other aerospace applications.

Real-Time Telemetry System for Lunar Missions

The Iris Lunar Rover project aimed to design advanced mission-critical systems for lunar exploration, emphasizing reliability and performance in harsh, remote environments. As part of the mission, I developed a real-time telemetry system to monitor the rover’s health and predict potential failures during critical operations.

Key Contributions:

  • Telemetry Interface Design: Created a front-end system providing real-time data insights for quick diagnostics and informed decision-making during missions.
  • Predictive Failure Analysis: Developed algorithms to forecast subsystem malfunctions, reducing downtime risks.
  • Software-Hardware Integration: Collaborated with a multidisciplinary team to ensure seamless communication and data processing.

Impact:

  • Reliability: Achieved 95% system reliability in monitoring critical subsystems.
  • Operational Safety: Enhanced operational safety by identifying and addressing potential disruptions preemptively.
  • Scalability: Established a scalable telemetry framework adaptable for future planetary missions.
